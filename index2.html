Tribute to Jhansi Rani Laxmi Bai
(1828-1858)


Biography


Jhansi Rani Laxmi Bai was born on November 19, 1828 and was the queen of the Maratha-ruled Jhansi State, India. She was one of the leading figures of the Indian Rebellion of 1857 and is remembered as a symbol of resistance against British rule in India. Jhansi Rani Laxmi Bai was known for her bravery, intelligence, and leadership skills.
Led her army into battle during the Indian Rebellion of 1857.Established hospitals, schools and reformed the administration of Jhansi.Was a supporter of women's education and empowerment.
The legacy of Jhansi Rani Laxmi Bai continues to inspire people all over the world. She is remembered as a symbol of resistance and bravery, and her contributions to women's education and empowerment have had a lasting impact. Her bravery, intelligence, and leadership skills have made her a true hero of India's struggle for independence.
